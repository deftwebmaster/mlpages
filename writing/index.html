Writing

Essays on warehouse operations, systems thinking, and building tools that work.

  • Debugging Your Own Life

    Recurring personal failures aren't character flaws. They're system design issues. Trace backward, find the root cause, fix the condition—not the behavior.

  • Defaults Are Decisions

    The choices you make once shape every choice after. Most people inherit defaults instead of choosing them—and spend years paying the cost.

  • Friction Is Data

    When something feels hard, don't just push through—investigate. The resistance is telling you something about the system.

  • Knowing What to Ignore

    The skill nobody teaches. Filtering signal from noise isn't magic—it's pattern-matched judgment. Taste is just compressed experience.

  • Sexy UI Is a Philosophy

    Good interfaces don't prove themselves upfront. They appear simple, then reveal depth as the user demonstrates intent.

  • Ship Before It's Ready

    The version that exists beats the version that's perfect. Shipping isn't about lowering standards—it's about learning from reality instead of theory.

  • Solve It Once

    Most fixes are patches—they stop the pain but leave the problem intact. Real fixes eliminate the condition so you never solve it again.

  • The Myth of the Side Project

    The term "side project" is a hierarchy in disguise. If it makes you sharper, more capable, or harder to replace, it's not a side project—it's just unpaid.

  • The Smallest Useful Version

    Not MVP as startup buzzword. The literal smallest thing that does the job—and why it's usually smaller than you think.

  • What Warehouse Ops and Web Dev Have in Common

    Strip away the tools and the work is structurally identical—maintaining data integrity across imperfect systems operated by humans.
