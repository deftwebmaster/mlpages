A weapon learns to choose. A pattern learns to break.

Marrek Locke was a weapon for a shadow intelligence bureau—until the system he served betrayed him. When he discovers his assassination targets are innocent civilians eliminated to serve corporate interests, he makes a choice that transforms him from asset to enemy. Now, hunted across the Outer Rim, he must forge a new identity in the void between law and chaos.

KEY PERSONNEL

MARREK LOCKE
Former Section Operative
Protagonist

A weapon who learned to think. Trained to trust patterns over people, Marrek's greatest asset is his ability to read situations before they fully develop. His greatest liability: taking responsibility for people who didn't ask.

SAREN DOLE
Former Handler
Ally

The handler who treated operatives as people capable of moral reasoning. When Marrek was targeted for elimination, Saren made the choice that cost them everything. Now trapped in a suicide posting with no extraction protocols.

THANE VADRIC
Corporate Consolidator
Threat Level: Extreme

A true believer with fanatical conviction. He sees corporate consolidation as the only path to survival in the Outer Rim—and has lost the ability to distinguish between saving people and controlling them.

THE CREW
The Calculated Risk
Crew

Not employees—chosen family bound by shared survival. Tessa (pilot), Jalen (engineer), Aria (medic), and Kaed (wildcard). Each saved or backed by Marrek at personal cost. They follow him because he gives what the galaxy withholds: agency, dignity, purpose.

Universe Framework

THE COMPACT

◈

THE CORE

Wealthy, cosmopolitan, heavily monitored. Post-scarcity for basic needs masks extreme wealth concentration. Most citizens believe the system works.

◇

THE OUTER RIM

Where Compact presence thins to skeleton patrols. Corporate pseudo-states, criminal syndicates, and fierce independence fill the power vacuum.

○

THE FRINGE

Beyond nominal authority. Multi-week journeys from civilization. Here, the Compact's reach ends and the void begins.

▣

SECTION

The Veiled Section doesn't officially exist. Black-ops division conducting deniable operations. Controlled by the Oversight Trinode—three shadows who answer to no one.

⬡

JUMP GATES

Massive orbital structures enabling near-instantaneous travel. Every transit logged, every identity verified. The surveillance network disguised as infrastructure.

◐

THE CALCULATED RISK

Modified courier-class vessel. Military-grade stealth systems shouldn't exist on civilian hulls. Neither should the man who flies her.

From the Author

A NOTE ON THE WORK

This series grew from a love of strategic fiction—stories where characters win through thinking, not just firepower. Timothy Zahn's work taught me that competence is compelling, that moral complexity beats simple heroism, and that the most interesting weapons are the ones that learn to choose.

Marrek Locke is a character built from questions: What happens when a tool develops judgment? When patterns break? When the system's most effective weapon decides the system is wrong?

— Matt Livingston
